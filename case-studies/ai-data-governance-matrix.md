## 🚨 1. Regulatory Context & Structural Gaps
Under **Article 10** (Data and Data Governance) and **Article 13** (Transparency and Provision of Information to Users) of the **EU AI Act**, providers of high-risk AI models face massive structural liabilities if their training, validation, and testing datasets cannot be systematically verified. 

Traditional development pipelines treat data engineering and legal compliance as separate domains. This architectural gap results in un-auditable models, exposing enterprises to penalties of up to €35 million or 7% of global annual turnover.

This framework bridges that gap by mapping regulatory mandates directly to automated engineering checkpoints.

## 🔒 3. Immutable Audit Trail Configuration (Policy-as-Code)
To ensure compliance logs cannot be altered by developers or malicious actors, all telemetry data generated by the validation matrix must be funneled into a write-once, read-many (WORM) storage configuration.
